1. Income Documentation

Recent Pay Stubs
Last 30 days of pay stubs showing year-to-date income
W-2 Forms
Last 2 years of W-2 forms from all employers
Tax Returns (If Self-Employed)
Last 2 years of complete personal tax returns with all schedules
Profit & Loss Statement (If Self-Employed)
Year-to-date P&L for current year

2. Asset Documentation

Bank Statements
Most recent 2 months of all checking and savings accounts (all pages)
Investment Account Statements
Most recent statements for retirement accounts, stocks, bonds (401k, IRA, etc.)
Gift Letter (If Applicable)
Signed letter if using gift funds for closing costs

3. Property & Current Loan Information

Current Mortgage Statement
Most recent statement showing current balance and payment
Homeowners Insurance Declaration Page
Current policy showing coverage amounts and effective dates
Property Tax Bill
Most recent annual property tax statement
HOA Statement (If Applicable)
Current HOA fees and contact information

4. Personal Identification

Photo ID
Copy of driver's license or state-issued ID (front and back)
Social Security Card
Copy of Social Security card or verification letter

5. Additional Documentation (If Applicable)

Divorce Decree or Separation Agreement
If applicable, including any alimony or child support details
Bankruptcy or Foreclosure Papers
Discharge papers if applicable within the last 7 years
Rental Income Documentation
Lease agreements and proof of rental income if applicable
Explanation Letters
Letters of explanation for any large deposits, credit issues, or employment gaps
📌 Note: Your loan officer may request additional documentation based on your specific situation. Documents should be clear, complete, and recent.
